Project Description

My project consists of 2 single-player games and a kind of tracker. The two games are Rock, Paper, Scissors and Blackjack. The tracker produces a bar graph based upon the results of your Rock, Paper, Scissors results.

Instructions

The file I submitted contains 2 separate files that are meant to be run separately. I was unable to make a zip file, so I decided to mark each file with a comment instead. I had mentioned so in a comment on my submission on the HW server.

When you run Rock, Paper, Scissors, there will be a prompt asking you if you want to play. Simply enter any value. Then, proceed play according to the instructions: you will be asked to enter a choice. After entering a choice, you will be given a result. Continue playing until you don't want to. Then, simply enter "quit" when prompted, and your overall results will be brought to you in the form of a bar graph.

When you run Blackjack, you will be automatically dealt a hand. Simply hit H for a hit or S to stand. If you need help on how to play Blackjack, click here. (Please note that my version of Blackjack only allows hitting and standing. There are no things like side bets or insurance.)

How it works

Both games will require an input from the play. Simply follow the prompts and instructions, and try your best not to put in unacceptable or incorrect values.

Rock, Paper, Scissors takes an input (rock, paper, or scissors) from the player. Then, the program will randomly select rock, paper, or scissors. The results are then compared. Rock beats scissors, scissors beats paper, and paper beats rock. If both the player and the program select the same thing, it's a tie. Then, the result is added into the player's win, loss, or tie count (depending on the result). When the player decides to quit, a bar graph will be produced that shows the player how many wins, losses, and ties they obtained.

Blackjack generates a deck and then randomly selects 2 cards for the player and 2 for the dealer. The player then plays Blackjack according to the rules. Unfortunately, my version of Blackjack only allows hitting and standing as the only options. If the player hits, a card is given to them, and its value is added on to the player's score. If the player decides to stand, it's the dealer's turn. The dealer will hit if they have a score lower than 17, and the dealer will stand when they have 17 or higher. Then, the scores of the player and dealer are compared. The higher score wins!
